# Relationship Management — Best Practice Policy

## Policy Statement
<!-- sources: ITIL 4 Relationship Management §2.1, §2.4, FitSM-2 §PR7 CRM -->

The organization shall establish, maintain, and continually improve effective relationships with all stakeholders who contribute to, benefit from, or are affected by the delivery of its services. This shall be achieved through a common approach to relationship management that is integrated into the organization's culture — promoting shared goals, open and transparent communication, collaboration, and mutual respect. The organization shall actively identify and manage its stakeholders, conduct regular service reviews with customers, systematically handle complaints, measure and act on satisfaction, and prevent and resolve conflicts. Healthy internal relationships shall be recognized as a prerequisite for healthy external relationships — the organization shall invest in both with equal commitment.

## Objectives
<!-- sources: ITIL 4 Relationship Management §2.4 PSFs, FitSM-2 §PR7 CRM -->

1. Establish and continually improve an effective approach to relationship management that is adopted across the organization and embedded in its culture
2. Ensure effective and healthy relationships within the organization — between individuals, teams, and departments — that support collaboration and value co-creation
3. Ensure effective and healthy relationships between the organization and its external stakeholders — customers, users, suppliers, partners, regulators, and others
4. Understand customer needs, expectations, and satisfaction through regular service reviews, complaint management, and satisfaction measurement
5. Prevent and resolve conflicts promptly through established mechanisms, shared values, and mediation where needed
6. Identify and manage all significant stakeholders according to their influence and interest, ensuring appropriate engagement at every level

## Scope

This policy applies to all relationships that the organization maintains in the course of managing and delivering its services. It covers:

- All internal relationships — between teams, departments, individuals, and management levels
- All external service relationships — with customers, users, and their representatives
- All external non-service relationships — with suppliers, partners, regulators, industry associations, media, investors, communities, and other stakeholder groups
- All roles with responsibility for relationship management, from organizational leaders through to individual staff members who interact with stakeholders
- The organizational culture, values, and behaviour patterns that shape how relationships are conducted

## Principles

### P1. Collaboration Over Exchange
<!-- sources: ITIL 4 Relationship Management §2.2.1, §2.4.1 -->
The organization shall promote collaborative, communal relationships over purely transactional, exchange-based ones. Collaborative relationships — characterized by shared goals, mutual trust, and willingness to support without immediate reciprocation — are more effective and resilient, particularly in complex environments. While exchange-based relationships may be appropriate for some standardized service arrangements, the organization shall actively work toward higher levels of communality and collaboration wherever possible.

### P2. Open and Transparent Communication
<!-- sources: ITIL 4 Relationship Management §2.2.3 -->
All stakeholder interactions shall be conducted with openness and transparency. The organization shall recognize that communication is always a two-way process, that timing and frequency matter, that no single method works for everyone, and that the message is shaped by the medium. Communication channels and methods shall be adapted to the needs and preferences of each stakeholder group.

### P3. Stakeholder-Driven Engagement
<!-- sources: ITIL 4 Relationship Management §2.2.4, §2.4.3 -->
The level and method of engagement with each stakeholder shall be determined by their influence and interest, not by organizational convenience. Stakeholder maps shall be maintained and regularly reviewed to ensure that engagement approaches remain appropriate as stakeholders' positions evolve. High-influence, high-interest stakeholders shall receive the most intensive engagement.

### P4. Prevention Over Remediation
<!-- sources: ITIL 4 Relationship Management §2.2.2 -->
The organization shall invest in preventing relationship breakdowns rather than only responding to them. This includes establishing clear values and behaviour expectations, providing conflict resolution mechanisms, promoting psychological safety, and actively identifying and addressing toxic behaviours (criticism, defensiveness, contempt, and obstruction) before they damage trust and collaboration.

### P5. Healthy Internal Relationships First
<!-- sources: ITIL 4 Relationship Management §2.4.2 -->
Successful external relationships depend on successful internal relationships. The organization shall actively monitor and maintain the health of relationships between teams, departments, and individuals — recognizing that the organizational climate directly affects the quality of service delivered to customers and the effectiveness of engagement with all external stakeholders.

### P6. Relationship as a Journey
<!-- sources: ITIL 4 Relationship Management §2.2.5 -->
Relationships shall be managed as journeys that evolve through defined stages — from exploration and engagement through to co-creation and value realization. Each stage requires specific activities, behaviours, and communication patterns. The organization shall define and follow appropriate relationship models that guide stakeholder interactions through these stages consistently.

### P7. Continual Relationship Improvement
<!-- sources: ITIL 4 Relationship Management §3.2.1, §3.2.2 -->
The organization's approach to relationships and the health of individual relationships shall be subject to regular review and improvement. Lessons learned from relationship incidents, conflicts, stakeholder feedback, and satisfaction data shall drive updates to relationship principles, models, and practices. Relationship improvement actions shall be tracked through the continual improvement register.

## Mandatory Requirements

### Essential (All Tiers)

| ID | Requirement |
|----|------------|
| RELM-R01 | A customer and stakeholder database shall be maintained with current contact information for all service customers |
| RELM-R02 | Key stakeholders shall be identified and their engagement needs documented |
| RELM-R03 | Communication channels for customer engagement shall be defined and communicated (for service reviews, complaints, escalations) |
| RELM-R04 | Periodic service reviews shall be conducted with customers at defined intervals |
| RELM-R05 | A complaints handling process shall be in place to register, investigate, and resolve customer complaints |
| RELM-R06 | Customer satisfaction shall be measured at regular intervals and acted upon when satisfaction is insufficient |
| RELM-R07 | A relationship manager role shall be assigned with accountability for customer and stakeholder relationship health |

### Intermediate (T2+)

| ID | Requirement |
|----|------------|
| RELM-R08 | Stakeholder maps shall be maintained categorizing stakeholders by influence and interest, with defined engagement approaches for each quadrant |
| RELM-R09 | Relationship principles shall be formally documented and communicated to all staff as part of the organization's culture |
| RELM-R10 | Formal relationship models shall be developed for key stakeholder groups, defining engagement journey stages, activities, and behaviour patterns |
| RELM-R11 | Relationship agents shall be assigned for major customer and stakeholder relationships |
| RELM-R12 | Periodic relationship reviews shall be conducted to assess the health and effectiveness of key relationships, with findings feeding into continual improvement |
| RELM-R13 | Internal relationship health shall be actively monitored through climate assessments, employee feedback, or similar mechanisms |

### Advanced (T3)

| ID | Requirement |
|----|------------|
| RELM-R14 | A formal culture analysis shall be conducted to assess the organization's culture and its impact on relationships, with recommendations for cultural development |
| RELM-R15 | Non-service stakeholder relationships (regulators, media, industry associations, investors) shall be actively managed with defined engagement approaches |
| RELM-R16 | In multi-supplier environments, the relationship management approach shall be coordinated across service integration boundaries |
| RELM-R17 | The organization's overall relationship approach and models shall be subject to periodic review and adjustment based on measured outcomes and stakeholder feedback |

## Compliance and Review

- This policy shall be reviewed at least annually or following a significant change to the organization's strategy, culture, stakeholder landscape, or service portfolio
- Compliance with this policy shall be assessed through relationship reviews, stakeholder satisfaction data, and complaints analysis
- Non-compliance shall be escalated to the relationship manager and organizational leadership for remediation
- Exceptions to this policy require documented justification and approval from the relationship manager or senior leadership
