# Continual Improvement — Best Practice RACI Matrix

## How to Read This Matrix

- **R** — Responsible: performs the work
- **A** — Accountable: owns the outcome (exactly one per activity)
- **C** — Consulted: provides input before the activity
- **I** — Informed: notified after the activity

Roles are graduated by maturity tier. Activities marked T2+ or T3 only apply at those tiers. The continual improvement manager is accountable for the continual improvement practice overall, while improvement owners are accountable for individual initiatives. In smaller organizations, the continual improvement manager role may be part-time or combined with a coaching or quality management role.

## Notes

1. **Dual Accountability Model:** The continual improvement practice has a distinctive accountability structure. The continual improvement manager is accountable for the practice as a whole — maintaining the CIR, coordinating prioritization, and ensuring the approach is effective. Individual improvement owners are accountable for their specific initiatives — planning, implementing, and delivering results. For the "Approve and secure resources" activity, accountability is shared: the improvement owner is accountable for developing the business justification, while the senior manager is accountable for the approval decision.
2. **Role Combining:** In smaller organizations, the continual improvement manager role may be part-time or combined with coaching, quality management, or service management roles. As organizational proficiency in continual improvement increases, the dedicated role may be reduced. The improvement owner role is typically held by whoever is most appropriate for the specific initiative — team leaders, project managers, practice managers, or service owners.
3. **Continual Improvement Manager / CIR Administrator:** Accountable for managing the continual improvement practice. Maintains the CIR. Coordinates prioritization of improvement initiatives. Facilitates the embedding of continual improvement into organizational culture. Ensures the organization has the knowledge, skills, and tools for effective improvement. This role requires strong coaching skills, understanding of improvement methodologies, and the ability to advocate for improvement across the organization.
4. **Improvement Owner:** Accountable for implementing a specific improvement initiative from planning through to completion and evaluation. Develops business justifications, plans the initiative, facilitates implementation, and measures results. Responsibility for an initiative should not be shared across teams — each initiative has a single owner. May be any team member, manager, or specialist appropriate to the initiative's scope and subject matter.
5. **Senior Manager:** Provides strategic direction and models improvement behaviours. Approves significant improvement investments. Ensures continual improvement is embedded in organizational culture through role-modelling, reward structures, and updated expectations. Evaluates improvement results for strategic decisions. Senior management commitment is critical — if senior managers do not adopt the practice, others will not adopt it either.
6. **All Staff:** Not listed as a separate RACI role, but all staff throughout the organization participate in continual improvement by identifying and submitting improvement opportunities. Service provider staff at all levels — leaders and team members, as well as external stakeholders and customers — contribute to the "Identify and log" activity. The CIM coordinates and enables this broad participation.
